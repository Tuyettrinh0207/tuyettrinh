STAGHORN SUMAC LEMONADE Is the staghorn sumac poisonous? No only Its relatives are poisonous, such as the poison sumac (Rhus toxicodendron) This is called "Indian Lemonade", a drink that is rich in Vitamin C and refreshing in a hot summer day. First you have to pick the red cluster from the staghorn sumac tree and maybe spread them out on the table for a moment so that insects can fully escape. Then you start freeing the “seeds” from their cobs. Take a bowl and fill it with 1 liter of water and add the fruits. Now let it rest for 10 minutes. After 10 minutes, use a potato masher and stir the mixture well so that the fruits submerges. I let this mixture steep in the refrigerator for about 12 to 24 hours, but you can also try in between to see whether the taste is already pleasant for your taste. You can also add sugar or honey to sweeten the lemonade. Finally, strain the liquid through a fine mesh sieve to filter out the fruit hair. You can of course also experiment by adding some more lemon balm or mint.

Spring's Arrival: Ten Thousands Doors of April

April is like a book that carries the scent of our world, and tells a tale of secret doors, of love, adventure and new beginnings. April is like the spirit of the woods, you will find it while walking upstream along the small creek. April is almost less a month than an experience: I feel more like I was part of grander things, moved by the same current of life. Like a lighthouse at sea guiding one to safety, April is a tonic for me who feels the world is too much sometimes. Spring's Arrival - Ten Thousands Doors of April - Her 86m2 2.JPG Then the rain came. Then the clouds disappeared and a soft, pale lightness fell over my town, as if it had freed itself from the earth and turned towards the sun. April is a promise that May is bound to keep. April releases us from the deceiving March - the false spring, a period in late winter or early spring during which the weather is warm enough to deceive vegetation, causing plants and animals to awaken early from dormancy. Then followed by periods of freezing temperatures. When this happens, the plants can die. Pablo Neruda wrote.
