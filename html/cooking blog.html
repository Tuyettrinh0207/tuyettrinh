10 Simple Microgreens and Sprouts Dishes - Seed to Table

Last week we have learnt to grow microgreens & sprout together. They are full of vitamins, minerals and phytochemicals and are particularly easy to digest because carbohydrates, proteins and fats are broken down into their individual components. Some studies said the nutritional value of microgreens is four to forty times higher than their mature versions. Also, if you've been to a fine dining restaurant in, say, the last twenty years - you see that microgreens are an essential part of a fine dining chef’s arsenal these days. Oh the ant-sized leaves carefully placed on top of the dish. The funny thing about it is that we can grow them for cheap at home. So for the home cook, microgreens is a fun and simple way to add a bit of color to your casual dishes and can help take your dinner party to a more sophisticated level. So stay with me to create 10 simple dishes with microgreens & sprouts today!

Pumpkin Bread

Instructions:
1. Add yeast and white sugar to warm milk. Stir well. Allow 10 minutes for yeast to active.
2. Mix flour, salt, brown sugar, ground cinnamon. Pour into the activated yeast bowl. Then add pumpkin puree, butter, and egg. Knead until the dough is smooth, elastic and does not stick to your hands. Note: If you knead the dough by Kitchenaid, gradually increase the speed from low to high. Beat for about 5-7 minutes, then let the dough rest for 5-10 minutes and then continue to knead.
3. Brush the bowl evenly with a little oil. Cover and let it rise until the dough has doubled in size.
4. Gently take out the dough, divide it into 8 portions. Divide cream cheese into 8 parts. Knead through the dough, roll and flatten, put a piece of cream cheese and then grab the sides of the dough to create a ball, roll it gently so that the ball is round and the dough surface is smooth. Use a thread to tie around the dough to make the pumpkin shape.
5. Let the dough rest for 30 minutes or until it rises to 70%.
6. Pre-heat the oven at 170 °C for 15 minutes. Brush milk or egg wash on top of the dough, bake for 20 minutes. Cover with aluminum foil when the bread begins to lightly brown, it keep them from getting too dried out and burnt.
7. Remove the bread from the oven, you can brush butter on the surface to make the surface of the bread more shiny. Let it cool down and cut the thread.
